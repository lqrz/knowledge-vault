# Off-Policy Monte Carlo

Off-policy Monte Carlo methods learn about one policy from episodes generated by another policy.

The policy being evaluated or improved is the **target policy**. The policy used to generate data is the **behavior policy**. This separation lets the behavior policy keep exploring while the target policy can be greedy or otherwise less exploratory.

## Coverage

Off-policy Monte Carlo requires coverage: if the target policy can select an action in a state, then the behavior policy must assign nonzero probability to that action. Without coverage, some target-policy returns cannot be reconstructed from behavior-policy data.

In control, Sutton and Barto require the behavior policy to be soft so all actions can be sampled.

## Importance Sampling

Off-policy prediction uses [[monte-carlo-methods/importance-sampling|importance-sampling]] to correct for the mismatch between target-policy and behavior-policy probabilities along sampled trajectories.

The correction can have high variance because it multiplies probability ratios across time. Weighted importance sampling usually reduces variance relative to ordinary importance sampling, but off-policy Monte Carlo remains delicate, especially for long episodes and early states.

## Control

Off-policy Monte Carlo control combines:

- A soft behavior policy that explores.
- A target policy that is greedy with respect to the current action-value estimates.
- Weighted importance sampling to update `Q` from behavior-policy episodes.

Learning can be slow when behavior-policy actions diverge from the greedy target policy in long episodes, because later importance weights may become zero or very small.
