# Glossary

## SFT

Supervised fine-tuning. A post-training stage where a model is trained on curated input-output examples.

## RLHF

Reinforcement learning from human feedback. A family of methods that use human preference data to optimize model behavior.

## RLAIF

Reinforcement learning from AI feedback. Similar to RLHF, but preference or critique signals are generated by AI systems.

## DPO

Direct Preference Optimization. A preference optimization method that trains directly on preferred and rejected responses without training a separate reward model.

## GRPO

Group Relative Policy Optimization. A reinforcement learning method associated with reasoning-oriented post-training workflows. See [[grpo-loss]] for the loss components covered by the course transcript.[^grpo-loss-transcript]

## Advantage

A relative reward signal used to scale policy updates. In [[grpo-loss]], positive advantages reinforce generated tokens and negative advantages discourage them.[^grpo-loss-transcript]

## Importance Sampling

A method for estimating expectations under a target distribution using samples from a different behavior distribution by weighting samples with a likelihood ratio. See [[importance-sampling]].

## Reference Model

A frozen baseline model used to compare against the trainable policy model. See [[policy-and-reference-models]].[^grpo-loss-transcript]

## Policy Model

The model being updated during RL-style fine-tuning. In adapter-based setups, the trainable part may be LoRA weights attached to a frozen base model. See [[policy-and-reference-models]].[^grpo-loss-transcript]

## Completion Mask

A token mask that makes loss apply only to completion or assistant tokens, not prompt tokens. See [[completion-mask]].[^grpo-loss-transcript]

## Ratio Clipping

A stabilization technique that clamps per-token policy ratios within a bounded range before applying the reward signal. See [[ratio-clipping]].[^grpo-loss-transcript]

## KL Divergence Penalty

A loss penalty that discourages the trainable policy model from drifting too far from a reference model. See [[kl-divergence-penalty]].[^grpo-loss-transcript]

## Reward Model

A model trained to score outputs according to preference data or desired behavior.

## Continued Pretraining

Additional next-token-prediction training on domain-specific text after the base model has already been pretrained.

## PEFT

Parameter-efficient fine-tuning. A family of methods that adapt a model by training a small subset of parameters or added modules while leaving most base weights frozen.

## LoRA

Low-Rank Adaptation. A PEFT method that freezes base model weights and trains low-rank update matrices in selected layers.

## QLoRA

Quantized LoRA. A memory-efficient fine-tuning setup that trains LoRA adapters while the frozen base model is loaded in low-bit precision.

## Distillation

Training a smaller or cheaper model to imitate outputs or behavior from a stronger teacher model.
